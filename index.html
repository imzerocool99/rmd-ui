❓ Anticipated Questions & Answers
What is the difference between Cash Distribution and In-Kind Transfer?
Cash Distribution (Sell): assets are sold inside the IRA and cash proceeds are wired to the client's bank account. In-Kind Transfer: actual shares move unsold from the IRA to a taxable brokerage account at fair market value — the client keeps the investment. Both methods satisfy the IRS RMD requirement per IRS Pub. 590-B. The agent selects the appropriate strategy based on client preference and can handle both within the same session.
How does the live RMD recalculation work?
When the advisor changes any Action Select dropdown (Sell / Transfer / Hold), the app immediately recalculates how much of the RMD is covered by the remaining active assets. Hold excludes that asset's value from coverage. Both Sell and Transfer count toward the RMD. The RMD Coverage chart and plain-language status line update in real time — before the advisor submits — so they can see the impact of every override.
Why do the Tax Efficiency numbers change when I change an action?
The agent's tax savings come from selling loss positions first. If the advisor overrides a loss-making asset to Hold, that loss is no longer realized — reducing the tax offset. The Tax Efficiency panel recalculates the smart tax bill in real time using only the assets still marked Sell or Transfer, so the advisor immediately sees how much of the $1,211 savings they're giving up.
What are the source citation icons in the AI Explanation?
Each line in the AI Explanation has one or more clickable icons (📋💹🌱💰📖🔄⚠️) that link directly to the IRS or Investopedia page most relevant to that specific decision. For example, a line about tax-loss harvesting gets a 🌱 icon linking to Investopedia's tax-loss harvesting guide and a 💹 icon linking to IRS Topic 409. This gives compliance teams and clients a direct audit trail from the AI decision to the regulatory source.
Is this using real money / real trades?
This solution uses Alpaca Paper Trading — a simulated brokerage environment. No real orders are placed. The upgrade path to a live environment is straightforward: replace the paper trading API credentials with a live brokerage integration.
Where does the AI run — does client data leave the firm?
The AI (Ollama/Phi-3) runs 100% locally on the machine. No client data is sent to any cloud service. The language model is downloaded once and runs fully offline — meeting the most stringent data residency requirements.
Can advisors always override the agent?
Yes — by design. The agent recommends, the advisor decides. Every asset has an Action Select dropdown that the advisor can freely change before submitting. The system is built around human-in-the-loop: the agent does the analysis, the advisor retains full control and accountability. Nothing executes until the advisor clicks Execute.

📋 RMD Rules & Compliance
What is a Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)?
An RMD is the minimum amount the IRS requires you to withdraw from a Traditional IRA or employer-sponsored retirement plan each year, starting at age 73. The amount is calculated by dividing your prior year-end account balance by a life expectancy factor from the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. Failure to take the full RMD triggers a 25% excise tax on the shortfall (reduced to 10% if corrected promptly). IRS RMD FAQ ↗
How does the agent calculate the RMD amount?
The agent uses the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table (the same table the IRS publishes in IRS Pub. 590-B). It divides the prior year-end IRA balance by the applicable distribution period factor for the client's age. For example, at age 75 the factor is 24.6 — so a $500,000 IRA requires a $20,325 withdrawal. The calculation is shown on the Dashboard and cited with a link to the IRS source.
What is the difference between Cash Distribution and In-Kind Transfer?
Cash Distribution (Sell): Assets are sold inside the IRA and cash proceeds are sent to the client's bank account. The distributed amount is taxed as ordinary income in the year of distribution.

In-Kind Transfer: The actual shares (unsold) move from the IRA to the client's taxable brokerage account. The fair market value (FMV) on the transfer date counts toward the RMD and is taxed as ordinary income — but the client keeps the investment. The cost basis in the taxable account becomes the FMV on transfer date. This is explicitly permitted by IRS Pub. 590-B. IRS Pub. 590-B ↗
Does an In-Kind Transfer satisfy the full RMD requirement?
Yes — the FMV of the shares on the date of transfer counts toward the RMD, even though no shares are sold. The client still owes ordinary income tax on the FMV transferred. The advantage is the client retains the investment and does not realize any capital gain or loss at the time of transfer.
What happens if the RMD is not fully covered?
The RMD Coverage Analysis panel will show a ⚠️ shortfall in red with a plain-language warning. This can happen if the advisor sets too many assets to Hold. The IRS imposes a 25% excise tax on any shortfall (reduced to 10% if corrected within the correction window). The agent is designed to prevent this — but advisors can override, and the live recalculation makes any shortfall immediately visible.

💰 Tax Efficiency & Source Citations
What is tax-loss harvesting and how does the agent use it?
Tax-loss harvesting is the strategy of selling positions with unrealized losses to offset realized gains — reducing the overall tax bill. The agent scores every asset in the portfolio by its gain/loss position and selects loss-making or low-gain assets first. Compared to a naive approach (randomly selecting assets), this can save thousands of dollars per client per year. The Tax Efficiency panel shows the exact comparison side-by-side. Investopedia ↗

What if the client's portfolio doesn't have enough assets to cover the RMD?
The RMD Coverage Analysis panel shows the shortfall in red with the exact gap amount and a ⚠️ plain-language warning. In production the agent would escalate to the advisor and potentially suggest additional assets or partial liquidations. The agent covers this detection; automated escalation workflow is a planned enhancement.
What if the client's tax bracket is different from 24%?
This solution uses 24% as a demonstration baseline. The tax savings calculation is proportional — at a 32% bracket the tax savings from the same $1,211 gain offset would be even larger (~$1,614 saved). In production the exact marginal rate per client, including state tax and alternative minimum tax considerations, would be pulled from the firm's tax engine.
Does the agent consider wash sale rules when harvesting losses?
Wash sale awareness is a production enhancement. The agent demonstrates the tax-loss harvesting concept and quantifies the savings. In production, the agent would cross-check the 30-day wash sale window (IRS Section 1091) to ensure that recently purchased substantially identical securities do not disqualify the harvested loss.
📈 Reinvestment & Products
Why does the reinvestment tab show Same Bank options?
The agent is designed to keep assets within the firm's ecosystem wherever possible. The ⭐ Same Bank option on each product card represents the firm's own offering for that product type — no transfer is required, the relationship is maintained, and the firm retains the AUM. Two alternative institutions are shown for comparison and client choice transparency.
Why are Municipal Bonds highlighted for RMD clients?
Municipal Bond interest income is exempt from federal income tax (and often state tax). RMD distributions are taxed as ordinary income — so a client who just took a large taxable RMD distribution is in a higher-than-normal tax situation that year. Investing some of the proceeds in tax-exempt muni bonds directly offsets that income tax impact. The agent specifically surfaces this for clients in the 22%+ bracket.

🔴 Business Problem ▼
  • IRS mandates annual RMDs after age 73 — failure triggers a 25% excise tax on the undistributed amount.

  • RMD cash sitting in a checking account earns nothing — no reinvestment guidance is typically provided.
  • The process does not scale without proportional staffing increases.

✅ Solution Overview ▼
  1. Calculates the IRS-mandated RMD amount using the Uniform Lifetime Table for the client's age and prior year-end balance.
  2. Analyzes the full portfolio across 10 asset classes — scores each asset for tax efficiency, gain/loss, and concentration risk.
  3. Selects assets with losses or underperformance first to minimize tax impact (tax-loss harvesting). Shows side-by-side savings vs naive random selection.
  4. Two execution modes: Cash Distribution (sell assets → transfer proceeds to client's bank) and In-Kind Transfer (move shares to taxable brokerage at FMV — no immediate sale).
  5. Human-in-the-Loop: Advisor reviews every asset recommendation via Action Select dropdown (Sell / Transfer / Hold). Live RMD coverage and tax impact recalculate instantly on each change before submission.
  6. AI explanation panel with per-line IRS / Investopedia source citations (🌱💹💰📋 icons) for every selected and protected asset.
  7. Advisory Intelligence strip below each panel — real-time market catalysts, AI signals, and firm product recommendations to support advisor decision-making.
  8. Reinvestment engine suggests 7 product types (HYSA, CD, T-Bills, Muni Bonds, Dividend ETF, Fixed Annuity, Money Market) with yield, risk, institution options, and annual income projection.
  9. Context-aware AI chatbot — reads the active tab and injects relevant context. 💬 Discuss buttons on asset rows pre-load that asset's rationale into the bot.
  10. Full audit trail: every agent run, advisor override, and execution action is timestamped and logged.

⚙️ Agent Execution Flow — SENSE → THINK → PLAN → ACT → REPORT ▼
1
SENSE — Reads client age, IRA balance, portfolio (13 assets / 10 classes), market prediction, and advisor mode preference (cash / in-kind).
2
THINK — Calculates RMD via IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. Scores every asset: losses first, then underperformers, then concentrated positions. Generates analyst intelligence signals per asset.
3
PLAN — Allocates exact quantities to match the RMD. Runs naive vs optimised tax comparison. Generates 7 reinvestment suggestions and AI explanation with IRS source citations. All in parallel.
4
ACT (Human-in-the-Loop) — Advisor reviews per-asset recommendations, adjusts Action Select dropdowns, sees RMD coverage and tax impact update live, then approves. Agent executes via Alpaca Paper Trading API.
5
REPORT — Displays results across all tabs. Logs every action with timestamps. Injects full context into the floating AI chatbot for follow-up questions.
Total runtime: ~4 seconds  |  Rule-based logic (RMD, tax, trade): < 0.1s  |  Ollama AI advice (async, phi3): ~4s with instant rule-based fallback
