❓ Anticipated Questions & Answers

          

            

              
What is the difference between Cash Distribution and In-Kind Transfer?

              
The system supports four strategies — all satisfy the IRS RMD requirement per IRS Pub. 590-B:

Cash Distribution: Assets are sold inside the IRA and cash is wired to the client's bank account.
In-Kind Transfer: Shares move unsold from the IRA to a taxable brokerage account at fair market value — the client keeps the investment.
QCD Donate: Up to $105,000/yr can be transferred directly to a qualified charity tax-free — the distribution counts toward the RMD and is excluded from taxable income.
Hold: Asset is excluded from the current RMD run (advisor override).

            

            

              
How does the live RMD recalculation work?

              
When the advisor changes any Action pill (Cash / In-Kind / QCD / Hold), the app immediately recalculates how much of the RMD is covered by the remaining active assets. Hold excludes that asset's value from coverage. Cash, In-Kind, and QCD all count toward the RMD total. The RMD Coverage chart and plain-language status line update in real time — before the advisor submits — so they can see the impact of every override.

            

            

              
Why do the Tax Efficiency numbers change when I change an action?

              
Tax savings come from selling loss positions first. If the advisor overrides a loss-making asset to Hold, that loss is no longer realized — reducing the tax offset. The Tax Efficiency panel recalculates the smart tax bill in real time using only the assets still marked Cash, In-Kind, or QCD, so the advisor immediately sees how much of the $2,220 savings they're giving up.

            

            

              
What are the source citation icons in the AI Explanation?

              
Each line in the AI Explanation has one or more clickable icons (📋💹🌱💰📖🔄⚠️) that link directly to the IRS or Investopedia page most relevant to that specific decision. For example, a line about tax-loss harvesting gets a 🌱 icon linking to Investopedia's tax-loss harvesting guide and a 💹 icon linking to IRS Topic 409. This gives compliance teams and clients a direct audit trail from the AI decision to the regulatory source.

            

            

              
Is this using real money / real trades?

              
This solution uses the Alpaca Brokerage Platform in paper trading mode — a simulated environment with realistic market data. No real orders are placed and no real money is at risk. The upgrade path to live is straightforward: swap the paper trading credentials for live brokerage credentials.

            

            

              
Where does the AI run — does client data leave the firm?

              
The AI (Ollama/Phi-3) runs 100% locally on the machine. No client data is sent to any cloud service. The language model is downloaded once and runs fully offline — meeting the most stringent data residency requirements.

            

            

              
Can advisors always override the AI recommendations?

              
Yes — by design. The AI recommends, the advisor decides. Every asset has an Action pill (Cash / In-Kind / QCD / Hold) that the advisor can freely change before submitting. The system is built around human-in-the-loop: the AI does the analysis, the advisor retains full control and accountability. Nothing executes until the advisor clicks Execute Plan.

            

            

              
How accurate is the tax savings calculation?

              
This solution uses a 24% marginal rate as a demonstration baseline. 📋 RMD Rules & Compliance

          

            

              
What is a Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)?

              
An RMD is the minimum amount the IRS requires you to withdraw from a Traditional IRA or employer-sponsored retirement plan each year, starting at age 73. The amount is calculated by dividing your prior year-end account balance by a life expectancy factor from the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table. Failure to take the full RMD triggers a 25% excise tax on the shortfall (reduced to 10% if corrected promptly). IRS RMD FAQ ↗

            

            

              
How is the RMD amount calculated?

              
The system uses the IRS Uniform Lifetime Table (published in IRS Pub. 590-B). It divides the prior year-end IRA balance by the applicable distribution period factor for the client's age. For example, at age 75 the factor is 24.6 — so a $500,000 IRA requires a $20,325 withdrawal. The calculation is shown on the Dashboard and cited with a link to the IRS source.

            

            

              
What is the difference between Cash Distribution and In-Kind Transfer?

              
Cash Distribution (Sell): Assets are sold inside the IRA and cash proceeds are sent to the client's bank account. The distributed amount is taxed as ordinary income in the year of distribution.

In-Kind Transfer: The actual shares (unsold) move from the IRA to the client's taxable brokerage account. The fair market value (FMV) on the transfer date counts toward the RMD and is taxed as ordinary income — but the client keeps the investment. The cost basis in the taxable account becomes the FMV on transfer date. This is explicitly permitted by IRS Pub. 590-B. IRS Pub. 590-B ↗

            

            

              
What is a Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD) and how does it help?

              
A QCD is a direct transfer from a Traditional IRA to a qualified charity. Up to $105,000/yr (2024 limit, indexed annually) can be donated this way. The key benefit: the amount transferred is excluded from taxable income entirely — it counts toward the RMD but is never added to the client's gross income. For charitably inclined clients in the 22%+ bracket, a QCD can be significantly more tax-efficient than taking a cash distribution and donating separately. IRS QCD Rules ↗

            

            

              
Does an In-Kind Transfer satisfy the full RMD requirement?

              
Yes — the FMV of the shares on the date of transfer counts toward the RMD, even though no shares are sold. The client still owes ordinary income tax on the FMV transferred. The advantage is the client retains the investment and does not realize any capital gain or loss at the time of transfer.

            

            

              
What happens if the RMD is not fully covered?

              
The RMD Coverage Analysis panel will show a ⚠️ shortfall in red with a plain-language warning. This can happen if the advisor sets too many assets to Hold. The IRS imposes a 25% excise tax on any shortfall (reduced to 10% if corrected within the correction window). The system is designed to prevent this — but advisors can override, and the live recalculation makes any shortfall immediately visible before submission.

Does the system execute trades automatically without advisor approval?

💰 Tax Efficiency & Source Citations

          

            

              
What is tax-loss harvesting and how does the AI use it?

              
Tax-loss harvesting is the strategy of selling positions with unrealized losses to offset realized gains — reducing the overall tax bill. The system scores every asset in the portfolio by its gain/loss position and selects loss-making or low-gain assets first. Assets with large unrealized gains are routed to In-Kind Transfer or QCD instead, avoiding a taxable event entirely. Compared to a naive approach (selling highest-gain assets first), this saved Robert $2,220 in this demo. The Tax Efficiency panel shows the exact comparison side-by-side. Investopedia ↗

🔄 Strategy & Edge Cases

          

            

              
Can the advisor mix strategies in the same RMD run?

              
Yes — within the same run, the advisor can override individual assets independently. For example, the AI might recommend Cash for loss positions, In-Kind for moderate gainers, and QCD for a charitable client. The advisor can change any individual row — switch one asset from Cash to Hold, another from In-Kind to QCD. All active rows count toward the RMD. The RMD Coverage panel reflects the combined total immediately.

            

            

              
What if the client's portfolio doesn't have enough assets to cover the RMD?

What if the client's tax bracket is different from 24%?

              
This solution uses 24% as a demonstration baseline. The tax savings calculation is proportional — at a 32% bracket the tax savings from the same $2,220 gain offset would be even larger (~$2,960 saved). In production the exact marginal rate per client, including state tax and alternative minimum tax considerations, would be pulled from the firm's tax engine.

            

            

              
Does the system consider wash sale rules when harvesting losses?

              
Wash sale awareness is a production enhancement. The AI demonstrates the tax-loss harvesting concept and quantifies the savings. In production, the system would cross-check the 30-day wash sale window (IRS Section 1091) to ensure that recently purchased substantially identical securities do not disqualify the harvested loss.

            

          

        


        

          
📈 Reinvestment & Products

          

            

              
How does the Reinvestment tab keep AUM within the firm?

              
The system is designed to keep distributed cash within the firm's ecosystem wherever possible. Each reinvestment product card represents a firm-managed offering — High-Yield Savings, CDs, Treasury Bills, Municipal Bonds, Dividend ETFs, Fixed Annuities, and Money Market funds. The advisor selects products, sees projected annual income update live, then clicks Deploy Funds to initiate the digital authorization workflow. No external transfer is required, the client relationship is maintained, and the firm retains the AUM.

            

            

              
Why are Municipal Bonds highlighted for RMD clients?

              
Municipal Bond interest income is exempt from federal income tax (and often state tax). RMD distributions are taxed as ordinary income — so a client who just took a large taxable RMD distribution is in a higher-than-normal tax situation that year. Investing some of the proceeds in tax-exempt muni bonds directly offsets that income tax impact. The system specifically surfaces this for clients in the 22%+ bracket.

✅ Stage 1 — Built & Running Today: Custom TF-IDF RAG

                  
A custom TF-IDF cosine similarity engine searches an in-memory institutional knowledge base to retrieve the most relevant firm knowledge for every AI response — no external framework, no pip install, runs inside the JAR.

                  

                    What is TF-IDF?  
                    TF (Term Frequency) scores how often a keyword appears in a document.
                    IDF (Inverse Document Frequency) down-weights common words and boosts rare, meaningful ones.
                    Combined, TF-IDF ranks every document by relevance to the query — then cosine similarity picks the closest match.
                    Result: the right firm knowledge surfaces for every advisor question, with zero cloud dependency.
